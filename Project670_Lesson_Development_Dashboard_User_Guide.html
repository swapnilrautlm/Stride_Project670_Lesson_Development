Project 670 — Delivery Plan (Lesson Development)

A walk-through of every section of the dashboard, what each number means, and how to find the slice of data you need.

1 What this dashboard shows

The dashboard answers the question stakeholders ask most often: "When are we delivering what, and to whom?"

It focuses on the final delivery milestone — "Deliver and SO" (LearningMate) — for every course in Project 670, broken down by State, Subject, Grade, and Derivative type.

Every chart, table, and calendar on the page reads from the same underlying course list (1,000 courses across 4 subjects and 30 MPS states), so picking a filter at the top instantly recalculates everything below.

2 Opening the dashboard

  1. Open the dashboard by clicking the link your administrator shared with you. The dashboard loads directly in your browser (Chrome, Edge, or Safari recommended).
  2. Wait a moment for the page to load. The header reads Project 670 Dashboard · Lesson Development · Status & Risk.
  3. If a section looks empty when the page first loads, scroll up and click the Reset button on the right side of the filter bar — it clears any stale filter state that may have been saved during a previous session.
Tip: Force-refresh with Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac) any time the dashboard looks stuck — that forces the browser to re-load the page and discard any cached versions.

3 The big picture

The page is organized top-to-bottom in this order:

  1. Brand bar — Stride wordmark, the project title, and the LearningMate co-mark on the right.
  2. Top navigation — currently a single tab, Delivery Plan, showing the active section.
  3. Filter bar (sticky) — stays glued to the top of the viewport as you scroll so you can refine the data from anywhere on the page.
  4. Delivery Window — 5 headline KPI tiles (Start, End, Window, Total, Busiest Day).
  5. By Month · Subject Breakdown — one card per month with subject mix bars.
  6. Daily Calendars — one full calendar grid per month with per-day delivery counts.
  7. Deliveries by Month — a tab card showing State × Month, Subject × Month, or Grade Group × Month cross-tabs.
  8. Delivery Schedule · Expandable List — a single flat table with date / state / subject grouping tabs and a Copy-to-Excel button.

Subject

Click to open, hold Ctrl (Windows) or Cmd (Mac) and click to select multiple subjects. The default is All subjects (Math, ELA, Science, Social Studies).

State

Single-select dropdown showing all 30 MPS states. Pick one to drill into a single state's delivery plan, or leave on All states.

Grade group (multi-select with grouped buckets)

This dropdown has two sections:

  • Grouped buckets at the top — pick these to slice by delivery stream:
    • Grade 3-5 (group) — all elementary courses across Grade 3, 4, and 5
    • Grade 6-12 SEM A (group) — every Semester A delivery for Grade 6 through Highschool
    • Grade 6-12 SEM B (group) — every Semester B delivery for Grade 6 through Highschool
  • Individual grades below — Grade 3, Grade 4, …, Grade 8, Highschool. Pick a single grade or hold Ctrl/Cmd and click several.
Tip: To see everything except elementary, hold Ctrl/Cmd and click both Grade 6-12 SEM A and Grade 6-12 SEM B. To see a specific grade like just Grade 7, click Grade 7 in the individual list (don't select the grouped buckets).

Derivative

Three options:

  • All — every course (1,000 courses)
  • Master only — only courses tagged "Master" in the spreadsheet (117 courses)
  • Derivatives only — everything else (60%, 80%, 40% variants — 883 courses)

Reset

Restores all filters to their defaults (all subjects, all states, all grade groups, all derivatives). Use this any time the dashboard looks unexpectedly empty.

How to read it

  • Group header rows (the light-blue rows with bold text) separate sections. They show the group name (a date, state, or subject), the count, and a colored pill with the total.
  • Course rows below each header show: the delivery date, subject pill, state, grade, type (Sem / Full Year), derivative (Master in blue, or 60% / 80% / 40%), course name (bold), and course code.
  • The first column displays the date in bold when grouping by State or Subject (because date varies within the group). When grouping by Date, dates are dimmer in the body rows since the date is already in the header.

The three grouping tabs

  • By Delivery Date (default) — One section per unique delivery day, sorted chronologically. Best for "what are we shipping next Friday?".
  • By State — One section per state, sorted by total volume. Best for stakeholder calls with a single state.
  • By Subject — Four sections (Math, ELA, Science, Social Studies). Best when reviewing subject-team workload.

Search

Type into the Search rows… box to filter rows in real time. The search matches against date, state, subject, course name, course code, derivative, and Sem A/B. For example, type algebra to see only Algebra courses, or louisiana grade 7 to find Louisiana's Grade 7 deliveries.

10 Copying to Excel / CSV

Two buttons next to the search box let you take the current view out of the browser.

Copy to Excel

Click Copy to Excel. The current filtered view is copied to your clipboard as tab-separated values. Open Excel (or Google Sheets), click any empty cell, and press Ctrl+V (Windows) or Cmd+V (Mac). Each course becomes one row with these columns:

  • Delivery Date, Weekday, Subject, State, Grade, Grade Group, Type, Sem, Derivative, Course Name, Course Code, Focus Course Name
Tip: The clipboard contains every row that's currently visible after filters and search. So you can filter to e.g. Math + State=Florida + Grade 6-12 SEM A, then click Copy to Excel and only those rows land in your spreadsheet.

CSV

Click CSV. The same data downloads as a properly-quoted CSV file with a date-stamped filename (Project670_Delivery_Schedule_2026-05-13.csv). Works even when clipboard permissions are restricted.

11 Common tasks & FAQ

"When does state X finish delivering?"

  1. Set the State filter to that state.
  2. Look at the End Date tile under Delivery Window.
  3. Optionally open the Delivery Schedule section and scroll to the last group header for that state to see the exact courses.

"Which subject is heaviest in July?"

  1. Scroll to Deliveries by Month and click the Subject × Month tab.
  2. Read down the Jul 26 column to find the largest count.

"Show me all Grade 6-12 Sem A deliveries"

  1. Open the Grade group filter.
  2. Click Grade 6-12 SEM A (group) in the Grouped buckets section.
  3. Everything below re-renders to show only that slice.

"I need to send today's delivery list to a colleague"

  1. Apply your filters.
  2. Optionally type today's date into the Search rows… box to limit further.
  3. Click Copy to Excel, paste into an email, or CSV to attach a file.

"Master vs Derivatives — what's the difference?"

In the source spreadsheet, each course is tagged with a Derivatives column: Master, 60%, 80%, or 40%. The Derivative filter exposes two shortcuts:

  • Master only — courses tagged Master (the canonical source course). 117 courses.
  • Derivatives only — every other course (the percentage variants derived from Masters). 883 courses.

"The dashboard looks empty after I changed something"

Click Reset in the filter bar. Your filter combination may have produced zero matches — Reset clears everything to defaults.

"I need a printout"

Use your browser's Print function (Ctrl+P / Cmd+P). The dashboard has a print stylesheet that hides interactive elements and lays the content out cleanly across pages. From the Print dialog, choose Save as PDF to get a portable file.
